# Notes for problem 002

Topic:
Partially Linear Single Index Model.

Source image:
\[
Y=X^\top\beta+g(Z^\top\alpha)+\epsilon,
\qquad
E(\epsilon\mid X,Z)=0.
\]

Parameter:
\[
\theta=(\beta^\top,\alpha^\top)^\top.
\]

Preferred mode:
Research mode / restricted semiparametric model.

Why this is hard:
- \(g\) is an unknown infinite-dimensional nuisance function.
- \(\alpha\) needs an identifiability normalization, such as \(\|\alpha\|=1\).
- The EIF is not obtained by ordinary least squares residuals without projecting out the nuisance tangent space generated by \(g\), the covariate law, and the conditional error law subject to \(E(\epsilon\mid X,Z)=0\).
- A full efficiency derivation may require smoothness of \(g\), nonsingularity of an information/operator matrix, and careful local parameterization of \(\alpha\).

Expected workflow:
1. Normalize the target: decide whether the finite parameter is \((\beta,\alpha)\) under a constraint or \((\beta,\gamma)\) under a local parameterization \(\alpha(\gamma)\).
2. Write the model:
   \[
   E\{Y-X^\top\beta-g(Z^\top\alpha)\mid X,Z\}=0.
   \]
3. Identify nuisance components:
   - unknown \(g\);
   - distribution of \((X,Z)\);
   - conditional error distribution subject to mean zero;
   - possibly conditional variance or score functions if an efficiency bound is pursued.
4. Characterize the tangent space and nuisance tangent space.
5. Derive candidate estimating equations and influence functions.
6. Project onto the orthocomplement of the nuisance tangent space before calling the result an EIF.
7. Clearly mark unresolved steps if the full projection is not completed.

Important warning:
Do not call a naive estimating-equation influence function efficient unless the nuisance projection has been derived and verified.
