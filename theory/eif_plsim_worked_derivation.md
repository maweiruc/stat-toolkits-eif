# Worked derivation: Partially Linear Single Index Model

This note gives a worked semiparametric efficiency derivation for the partially linear single index model

\[
Y=X^\top\beta+g(Z^\top\alpha)+\varepsilon,
\qquad
E(\varepsilon\mid X,Z)=0.
\]

The purpose is to illustrate a research-mode derivation where the main work is not formula lookup, but tangent-space decomposition and nuisance projection.

---

## 1. Model and parameterization

Observed data:

\[
O=(Y,X,Z),
\qquad
W=(X,Z).
\]

Let

\[
T=Z^\top\alpha,
\qquad
\mu(W)=X^\top\beta+g(T),
\qquad
\varepsilon=Y-\mu(W).
\]

The model assumes

\[
E(\varepsilon\mid W)=0.
\]

Because \(g(Z^\top\alpha)\) is invariant to scale changes of \(\alpha\), \(\alpha\) must be normalized. One convenient approach is to use a local unconstrained parameterization

\[
\alpha=\alpha(\gamma),
\qquad
\gamma\in\mathbb R^{q-1},
\]

and define

\[
\vartheta=(\beta^\top,\gamma^\top)^\top.
\]

Let

\[
J_\gamma=\frac{\partial \alpha(\gamma)}{\partial\gamma^\top},
\qquad
Z_\gamma=J_\gamma^\top Z.
\]

Under a delete-one-component parameterization, \(Z_\gamma\) is often written as \(Z_{-1}\). Under a unit-norm parameterization, \(Z_\gamma\) includes the Jacobian induced by the constraint.

---

## 2. Density factorization

Write the joint density as

\[
p(o)
=
p_{X,Z}(x,z)\eta(\varepsilon,x,z),
\]

where

\[
\varepsilon=y-x^\top\beta-g(z^\top\alpha).
\]

Let

\[
\ell_\varepsilon(\varepsilon,w)
=
\frac{\partial}{\partial\varepsilon}\log \eta(\varepsilon,w),
\qquad
\rho(\varepsilon,w)=-\ell_\varepsilon(\varepsilon,w).
\]

The mean-zero restriction implies, under standard regularity conditions,

\[
E(\varepsilon\mid W)=0,
\qquad
E\{\rho(\varepsilon,W)\varepsilon\mid W\}=1.
\]

Define

\[
\sigma^2(W)=E(\varepsilon^2\mid W).
\]

---

## 3. Nuisance tangent spaces

The nuisance tangent space has three parts.

### 3.1 Covariate-law tangent space

\[
\Lambda_{X,Z}
=
\{a(X,Z):E\{a(X,Z)\}=0\}.
\]

### 3.2 Conditional error-law tangent space

Perturbations of the conditional error distribution must preserve normalization and the conditional mean-zero restriction:

\[
\Lambda_{\varepsilon\mid X,Z}
=
\left\{
a(\varepsilon,X,Z):
E(a\mid X,Z)=0,\,
E(a\varepsilon\mid X,Z)=0
\right\}.
\]

### 3.3 Unknown link-function tangent space

A perturbation \(g_t(t)=g(t)+t a(t)\) changes the residual by \(-t a(T)\), producing a score in the span

\[
\Lambda_g
=
\{\rho(\varepsilon,W)a(T):a \text{ square-integrable}\}.
\]

After projecting out the conditional error-law nuisance, this component can be represented as

\[
\widetilde{\Lambda}_g
=
\left\{
\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}(W)a(T):a \text{ square-integrable}
\right\}.
\]

Thus the relevant nuisance space can be written as

\[
\Lambda
=
\Lambda_{X,Z}
\oplus
\Lambda_{\varepsilon\mid X,Z}
\oplus
\widetilde{\Lambda}_g.
\]

---

## 4. Raw parametric scores

For the local parameter \(\vartheta=(\beta^\top,\gamma^\top)^\top\), the derivative of the conditional mean is

\[
H(W)
=
\begin{pmatrix}
H_\beta(W)\\
H_\gamma(W)
\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
X\\
g'(T)Z_\gamma
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

The raw scores are

\[
S_\beta=\rho(\varepsilon,W)X,
\]

and

\[
S_\gamma=\rho(\varepsilon,W)g'(T)Z_\gamma.
\]

With the sign convention \(\rho=-\partial_\varepsilon\log\eta\), this matches the common form

\[
S_\beta
=
-\frac{\eta_\varepsilon'(\varepsilon,X,Z)}{\eta(\varepsilon,X,Z)}X,
\]

\[
S_\gamma
=
-\frac{\eta_\varepsilon'(\varepsilon,X,Z)}{\eta(\varepsilon,X,Z)}
g'(Z^\top\alpha)Z_\gamma.
\]

---

## 5. Projection away from the conditional error-law nuisance

The orthocomplement of \(\Lambda_{\varepsilon\mid X,Z}\) inside the conditional score space is spanned by \(\varepsilon\). Therefore

\[
\rho(\varepsilon,W)H(W)
\quad\leadsto\quad
\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}(W)H(W),
\]

because

\[
E\{\rho(\varepsilon,W)\varepsilon\mid W\}=1.
\]

This gives the preliminary score after removing conditional error-shape nuisance:

\[
S_{\vartheta,0}
=
\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}(W)H(W).
\]

This is not yet efficient because it may still have a component in \(\widetilde{\Lambda}_g\), the nuisance space generated by the unknown \(g\).

---

## 6. Projection away from the unknown \(g\)

The nuisance space generated by \(g\) is

\[
\widetilde{\Lambda}_g
=
\{\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}(W)a(T)\}.
\]

Project \(H(W)\) onto functions of \(T\) using the weighted inner product

\[
\langle u,v\rangle_w
=
E\{\sigma^{-2}(W)u(W)v(W)^\top\}.
\]

The weighted projection is

\[
\Pi(H\mid T)
=
\frac{
E\{\sigma^{-2}(W)H(W)\mid T\}
}{
E\{\sigma^{-2}(W)\mid T\}
}.
\]

Define

\[
H^\perp(W)
=
H(W)-\Pi(H\mid T).
\]

Then

\[
E\{\sigma^{-2}(W)H^\perp(W)\mid T\}=0,
\]

so

\[
\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}(W)H^\perp(W)
\]

is orthogonal to \(\widetilde{\Lambda}_g\).

---

## 7. Efficient scores

The efficient score for \(\vartheta\) is

\[
S_{\vartheta,\mathrm{eff}}(O)
=
\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}(W)H^\perp(W).
\]

Equivalently, block by block:

\[
S_{\beta,\mathrm{eff}}
=
\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}(X,Z)
\left[
X
-
\frac{
E\{\sigma^{-2}(X,Z)X\mid Z^\top\alpha\}
}{
E\{\sigma^{-2}(X,Z)\mid Z^\top\alpha\}
}
\right],
\]

and

\[
S_{\gamma,\mathrm{eff}}
=
\varepsilon g'(Z^\top\alpha)\sigma^{-2}(X,Z)
\left[
Z_\gamma
-
\frac{
E\{\sigma^{-2}(X,Z)Z_\gamma\mid Z^\top\alpha\}
}{
E\{\sigma^{-2}(X,Z)\mid Z^\top\alpha\}
}
\right].
\]

Under a delete-one-component parameterization, replace \(Z_\gamma\) by \(Z_{-1}\).

---

## 8. Efficient information and EIF

The efficient information matrix is

\[
I_{\mathrm{eff}}
=
E\{S_{\vartheta,\mathrm{eff}}S_{\vartheta,\mathrm{eff}}^\top\}
=
E\left[
\sigma^{-2}(W)H^\perp(W)H^\perp(W)^\top
\right].
\]

If \(I_{\mathrm{eff}}\) is nonsingular, the EIF for the local parameter \(\vartheta\) is

\[
\boxed{
D_\vartheta(O)
=
I_{\mathrm{eff}}^{-1}
S_{\vartheta,\mathrm{eff}}(O)
=
I_{\mathrm{eff}}^{-1}
\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}(W)H^\perp(W).
}
\]

For the constrained parameter \(\alpha=\alpha(\gamma)\),

\[
D_\alpha(O)=J_\gamma D_\gamma(O),
\]

where \(D_\gamma\) is the \(\gamma\)-block of \(D_\vartheta\). This ensures that \(D_\alpha\) lies in the tangent space of the constraint. For example, under the unit-norm constraint,

\[
\alpha^\top D_\alpha(O)=0.
\]

---

## 9. Homoskedastic simplification

If

\[
\sigma^2(W)\equiv\sigma^2,
\]

then the weighted projection reduces to ordinary conditional expectation:

\[
\Pi(H\mid T)=E(H\mid T),
\]

and

\[
H^\perp(W)=H(W)-E(H\mid T).
\]

Thus

\[
S_{\vartheta,\mathrm{eff}}
=
\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}\{H(W)-E(H\mid T)\}.
\]

---

## 10. Verification

### Mean zero

\[
E\{S_{\vartheta,\mathrm{eff}}\mid W\}
=
\sigma^{-2}(W)H^\perp(W)E(\varepsilon\mid W)
=0.
\]

Therefore the efficient score and the EIF are mean zero.

### Orthogonality to \(\Lambda_{X,Z}\)

Since

\[
E(S_{\vartheta,\mathrm{eff}}\mid W)=0,
\]

it is orthogonal to every \(a(X,Z)\in\Lambda_{X,Z}\).

### Orthogonality to \(\Lambda_{\varepsilon\mid X,Z}\)

For any \(a\in\Lambda_{\varepsilon\mid X,Z}\),

\[
E(a\mid W)=0,
\qquad
E(a\varepsilon\mid W)=0.
\]

Hence

\[
E\{S_{\vartheta,\mathrm{eff}}a\}
=
E\left[
\sigma^{-2}(W)H^\perp(W)E(\varepsilon a\mid W)
\right]
=0.
\]

### Orthogonality to \(\widetilde{\Lambda}_g\)

For any \(b(T)\),

\[
E\left[
\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}(W)H^\perp(W)
\cdot
\varepsilon\sigma^{-2}(W)b(T)
\right]
=
E\left[
b(T)E\{\sigma^{-2}(W)H^\perp(W)\mid T\}
\right]
=0.
\]

The last equality is exactly the weighted projection condition.

---

## 11. Regularity assumptions and status

The display above is the semiparametric EIF for the local parameter \(\vartheta=(\beta^\top,\gamma^\top)^\top\) under the stated model if:

- \(g\) is differentiable in a neighborhood of the true index;
- the local parameterization \(\alpha=\alpha(\gamma)\) is valid and identifiable;
- the conditional error density is regular enough for integration by parts;
- \(0<c<\sigma^2(W)<C<\infty\), or otherwise the weighted projections are well-defined;
- \(I_{\mathrm{eff}}\) is nonsingular;
- the weighted conditional expectations exist.

If these conditions are not established, the efficient score remains a candidate and the unresolved step is verification of regularity, invertibility, and tangent-space closure.
