# Restricted-model projection guide for EIFs

This guide explains how to handle efficient influence functions when the statistical model is restricted. It is meant to prevent the common error of deriving a full nonparametric influence function and calling it efficient under a smaller semiparametric model.

---

## 1. Core idea

Let \(\mathcal M\) be the statistical model and let \(\mathcal T_\mathcal M\) be its tangent space in \(L_0^2(P)\).

An influence function \(D\) for \(\psi(P)\) must satisfy

\[
\dot\psi_P(S)=E_P\{D(O)S(O)\}
\]

for every score \(S\in\mathcal T_\mathcal M\).

The efficient influence function is the canonical gradient:

\[
D_{\mathrm{EIF}}=\Pi_{\mathcal T_\mathcal M} D_{\mathrm{full}},
\]

where \(D_{\mathrm{full}}\) is a gradient in a larger nonparametric model and \(\Pi_{\mathcal T_\mathcal M}\) is the \(L_2(P)\) projection onto the restricted model tangent space.

If the projection is not performed, the expression should be called the full-model IF, not the restricted-model EIF.

---

## 2. Projection checklist

For a restricted-model EIF derivation, the agent must state:

```text
1. Larger model used to get D_full
2. Restricted model constraints
3. Tangent space T_M
4. Projection operation Pi_T(D_full)
5. Resulting EIF
6. Verification that EIF lies in T_M
7. Verification that D_full - EIF is orthogonal to T_M
```

---

## 3. Hilbert-space projection facts

All projections are in the inner product

\[
\langle f,g\rangle = E\{f(O)g(O)\}.
\]

The residual after projection must satisfy

\[
D_{\mathrm{full}}-\Pi_{\mathcal T}D_{\mathrm{full}}
\perp
\mathcal T.
\]

If \(\mathcal T\) is generated by a finite-dimensional score \(S_\theta(O)\), then the projection of any \(D\in L_0^2(P)\) onto \(\operatorname{span}(S_\theta)\) is

\[
\Pi(D)
=
E\{D S_\theta^\top\}
E\{S_\theta S_\theta^\top\}^{-1}
S_\theta.
\]

For a parametric model \(p_\theta\) and scalar target \(\psi(\theta)\),

\[
D_{\mathrm{EIF}}
=
\dot\psi_\theta^\top I_\theta^{-1}S_\theta(O),
\]

where \(S_\theta\) is the parametric score and \(I_\theta=E(S_\theta S_\theta^\top)\).

---

## 4. Product-law and conditional-law tangent spaces

Many observed-data laws factorize as

\[
p(o)=p(y\mid a,x)p(a\mid x)p(x).
\]

The full nonparametric tangent space decomposes into orthogonal components:

\[
\mathcal T
=
\mathcal T_{Y\mid A,X}
\oplus
\mathcal T_{A\mid X}
\oplus
\mathcal T_X.
\]

The components are characterized by:

\[
E(S_{Y}\mid A,X)=0,
\]

\[
E(S_A\mid X)=0,
\]

\[
E(S_X)=0.
\]

If a conditional law is known by design, its tangent component is removed. If a conditional law is parametric, its tangent component is replaced by the span or closure of its parametric score.

---

## 5. Known mechanism models

Examples:

- randomized treatment with known \(g(a\mid x)\)
- known missingness probability \(\pi(x)\)
- known sampling probability
- known censoring law

The tangent space excludes scores for the known mechanism.

Procedure:

1. Derive \(D_{\mathrm{full}}\) under the nonparametric observed-data model.
2. Compute the projection of \(D_{\mathrm{full}}\) onto the known-mechanism score space.
3. Subtract that component.

For a point-treatment factorization, the treatment-mechanism score space is

\[
\mathcal T_{A\mid X}
=
\{S_A(A,X): E(S_A\mid X)=0\}.
\]

The projection of \(D\) onto this space is

\[
\Pi_{\mathcal T_{A\mid X}}D
=
E(D\mid A,X)-E(D\mid X).
\]

If the treatment mechanism is known, the candidate projected IF is

\[
D_{\mathrm{known}\ g}
=
D_{\mathrm{full}}
-
\{E(D_{\mathrm{full}}\mid A,X)-E(D_{\mathrm{full}}\mid X)\}.
\]

For the usual ATE, this subtraction is zero because the full ATE IF is already orthogonal to treatment-mechanism scores. For targets that depend on the treatment mechanism, it may not be zero.

---

## 6. Parametric nuisance submodels

Suppose a conditional law has a parametric nuisance score \(S_\gamma\), so the tangent component is

\[
\mathcal T_\gamma=\operatorname{span}\{S_\gamma\}.
\]

If the full nonparametric component for that law is \(D_j\), replace it by its projection:

\[
D_{j,\mathrm{proj}}
=
E(D_j S_\gamma^\top)
E(S_\gamma S_\gamma^\top)^{-1}
S_\gamma.
\]

Then recombine the projected components.

Important: if the target depends on the nuisance parameter, include its derivative through \(\gamma\). Do not simply delete the component.

---

## 7. Independence restrictions

If the model imposes an independence restriction, the tangent space is smaller than the unrestricted joint-law tangent space.

Example:

\[
p(x,z)=p(x)p(z).
\]

Then scores decompose as

\[
S(X,Z)=S_X(X)+S_Z(Z),
\qquad
E(S_X)=E(S_Z)=0.
\]

The projection of a full-data gradient \(D(X,Z)\) onto this additive tangent space is

\[
\Pi(D)
=
E(D\mid X)+E(D\mid Z)-E(D).
\]

This pattern generalizes to product restrictions and can prevent an agent from using an unrestricted joint-law IF when independence is assumed.

---

## 8. Conditional moment restrictions

For models defined by conditional moments, such as

\[
E\{\rho(O;\eta)\mid X\}=0,
\]

the tangent space is the set of scores satisfying the derivative of the restriction. These projections are problem-specific.

The agent should not invent a projection. It should state:

```text
The full nonparametric IF can be derived, but the efficient IF under this conditional moment model requires projection onto the tangent space defined by the moment restriction.
```

Then derive the projection only if the tangent space and projection equations are clear.

---

## 9. Verification after projection

After proposing a restricted-model EIF \(D^\ast\), verify:

1. Mean zero:

\[
E(D^\ast)=0.
\]

2. Pathwise derivative identity for allowed scores:

\[
\dot\psi_P(S)=E(D^\ast S),\qquad S\in\mathcal T_\mathcal M.
\]

3. Membership:

\[
D^\ast\in\mathcal T_\mathcal M.
\]

4. Orthogonality of the residual:

\[
E\{(D_{\mathrm{full}}-D^\ast)S\}=0
\]

for every \(S\in\mathcal T_\mathcal M\).

---

## 10. Safe language

Use precise labels:

```text
Full nonparametric IF:
...

Restricted-model EIF after projection:
...
```

If projection is incomplete:

```text
The expression above is not yet the restricted-model EIF. It is the full-model IF, and the efficient restricted-model gradient requires projection onto the model tangent space.
```
